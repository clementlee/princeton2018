Welcome Princeton Class of 2018
blog lingo clubs
Hey, what's up. You just got into Princeton. And that's pretty awesome. We want to show you what it's like here. Let's give you a view of Princeton, by Princeton students.
Beast (n)
the beer Eating Clubs typically have on tap. Not so good you'll ever crave or miss it, but not so bad you'll mind it. Plus, free makes anything taste better.
Eating Clubs (n)
10 social organizations housed in separate buildings on Prospect Ave (The Street) with exclusively upperclassmen membership. Clubs provide 'meal plans' for members, as well as a variety of other social and engagement opportunities. They typically host parties at night (Thurs/Sat are popular nights) that are available to Princeton students - either through members or simply with your Princeton ID
Fire Inspection (n)
Twice a semester rooms are quickly inspected for fire-code violations.
Late Meal (n)
A time in which students can grab a late lunch or late dinner in Frist Campus Center using a meal swipe.
McCosh (n)
1. University Health Services building, located near Frist. 2. building that runs parallel the University Chapel and contains classrooms, lecture halls and offices.
RCA (n)
short for Residential College Adviser. This junior or senior lives near you. Will host study breaks and attend various orientation events with you.
Reading Period (n)
a 9-day period immediately preceding the period of Final Exams each semester when classes are typically not scheduled to allow for exam preparation and completing written work which is due on the final day of the Reading Period. NOTE: Midterms do not have a reading period preceding them.
Pass (n)
a business card sized slip issued by an Eating Club so that non-members can gain access for a night out. Each club has their own pass policy and design, but you'll figure it all out quickly.
PDF (v)
short for "Pass, D, or Fail," this is a grading option students can elect when taking a course. Instead of a letter grade on your transcript you get a P (pass), D, or F. Students can elect the PDF grading option for up to 4 classes through their Princeton careers. You cannot PDF more than one class per semester and you cannot PDF courses that fulfill your concentration requirements. Students often elect PDF when taking 5 courses or when fulfilling distribution requirements.
Pequod (n)
A course packet designed by a professor for a specific course that can be purchased, if part of the syllabus, on campus at the U-Store.
Precept (n)
1. group of 10-15 students in a class who meet once a week to discuss the course readings and lectures.
2. A place where certain students make verbose claims; don't be intimidated and speak up!
Prox (n/v)
1. the Princeton ID, PUID, or Tiger Card. This card gets you into buildings, counts for meals at the dining halls and Frist, and gets you into the Eating Clubs.
2. "to prox", to swipe one's card past a sensor to unlock a door. After hours, you may not prox into certain buildings unless you are taking a class in that building or a member of that particular department.
RCA (n)
short for Residential College Adviser. This junior or senior lives near you. Will host study breaks and attend various orientation events with you.
The Street (n)
Prospect Avenue, where the ten eating clubs are located
Zee Group (n)
short for Advisee Group. These are the fifteen to twenty students who live near you and share the same RCA.
